The Sonic Storehouse

The sound design for this video was an opportunity for experimentation and exploration of synchretic and diegetic sound. The coincidence of sonic and visual elements merges the elements together in the audienceâ€™s mind in 'synchresis', regardless of the rationality of the pairing. Many of the sounds used in the video are illogical or do not mirror reality, however they convey meaning seamlessly enough that their presence in the sound design goes unquestioned by audiences.

This video draws on such classic works as Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse (2018) and the BBC's Merlin (2008-2012) and recognises their mastery in applying both synchretic and diegetic sound techniques.

This three-part podcast was one of my first forays into sound design. The opening theme music is my original composition, however all other music was sourced online, as described in the credits.

The soundtrack to this video was designed to a brief to evoke the atmosphere of Halloween while remaining upbeat and inoffensive. The length of the video was undetermined at the time of composition, hence the repeating melody was ideal.

This sound work inquires into the ambiguity of digital piracy in a speculative future. Depicting a dial-up unauthorised library of sound files, the project draws parallels between historical archival practice and digital piracy along a potential future trajectory.

The work draws from algorithmic methodology at multiple points and implicitly and explicitly presents benefits and issues related to piracy, such as increasing accessibility and erasing artist credit, and positions the listener to be complicit in the act without prescriptively demanding they either embrace or renounce piracy themselves.
